After creating the brand concept and graphic artwork, I designed the website between July - August 2020. Although I used a CMS to build the website, I edited large sections of the HTML code in order to fully customise the appearance of the site and ensure it was mobile-responsive.

UI & UX Process

––CHALLENGE

to create a user-friendly, mobile-friendly e-commerce website that would:

  1. have a strong brand presence, reflecting the core values of the brand in the design of the website itself,

  2. pursuade new customers to purchase products and encourage repeat business from existing customers.

1. Understanding the Brand and Target Users

I identified the above characterstics, which I referred to whilst designing the website, in order to ensure the site reflected the brand's identity.

Target Users

For the first product launch, my target users were indiviudals aged between 18-30 living around London, as the majority of the followers on the Complements Only instagram were in this demographic. All marketing was carried out via instagram, so I expected most users to shop using their mobile phones.

2. Research

Competitor Analysis

I identified key competitors within the industry, such as Carhartt and Stussy, and then compiled a table that outlined how, what and where information was displayed throughout their websites. I focused on the home page in particular. My key findings were:

  • Preference for non text-heavy, image-led sites.
  • Cleanly designed websites, with lots of whitespace, were more compelling and satisfying to use.
  • Numerous links to products and product categories throughout the home page promoted sales.

User Interviews

I questioned individuals within my target demographic, in order to understand their wants, needs and expectations for an e-commerce website. I discovered that:

All users frequently purchased clothing through their mobiles.
Users favoured sustainable brands, however designs were the most important factor when making purchasing decisions.
Users favoured paypal checkout over manually entering card details and addresses.

Business Requirements

I noted down the high-level business requirements I needed each page of the website to fulfil:

Home
  • Make users want to buy products and showcase which products exist.
  • Provide key information about the company (such as the made-to-order process and availability of custom designs).
  • Make users want to find out more about the brand.
  • Provide quick links for getting in touch.
About
  • Make users aware of the brand's message and therefore also want to be aligned with those values.
  • Make users aware of the made-to-order process, so they are not surprised by longer delivery times.
  • Make users feel good about buying from the brand.
Shop
  • Show users what products exist.
  • Encourage users to purchase products.
  • Provide key information on sizing to maximise satisfaction after purchasing and minimise returns.
Gallery
  • Make users want to buy clothes by aligning themselves with the “complements only” lifestyle.
  • Support the product photos by enabling users to see what the clothes look like on.

3. Design

Wireframes

I began by sketching wireframes on paper and then moved on to creating lo-fi digital wireframes* (see right) using the app Procreate.

On this project, I did not think it was necessary to create hi-fi wireframes, due to the fact I was working alone and the CMS enabled me to produce and edit live content very quickly. For examples of hi-fi wireframes and prototypes, which I have created using the tool 'Sketch' please see previous project.

Palette

My designs and images to be used on the site were very colourful. So, I chose a neutral white-grey palette for the site.

4. Build

I built the site over a few days using DotGo's website builder. I tested the site on different devices, used media queries and changed column ordering within the Bootstrap grid, to ensure the site was fully responsive.

5. Evaluate

Usability Testing

I decided to test-launch the website first, offering a 10% discount to all users, in exchange for them providing me with feedback. I asked users about their journey through the website and to rank statements from 1-5, based on whether they "strongly agreed" or "strongly disagreed."
The statements included:

  • "I had to search around the site for a while to get the information I wanted."
  • "I found the pictures in the Gallery page helpful in deciding what to order."
  • "After seeing the website, I changed my mind about what I wanted to buy."
Results and Amendments

I was pleased that most users strongly agreed that information was easy to find, the site was easy to use and that the images used throughout the site supported the products. My key findings and actions taken were as follows:

  1. Key Finding: Although most users agreed that the pictures in the Gallery made them more inclined to buy products and caused some to change their purchasing decisions, many users did not visit the Gallery during their journey through the website.
    Action: I included a link to the Gallery in the top navigation bar.
  2. Key Finding: Although most users agreed that the environmental benefits of a made-to-order process made them more inclined to purchase products, many users failed to visit the About page before placing their order.
    Action: I included additional links to the made to order section (using an anchor) of the About page from the Home page.
  3. Key Finding: Some users highlighted that expected delivery times were not immediately clear.
    Action: I clarified expected delivery times in the "made-to order" section and at the checkout.

I plan to carry out further user testing and continually enhance and re-design the site, as and when I create further products.
